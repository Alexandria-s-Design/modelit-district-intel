## Key Talking Points

1. **"15% math proficiency demands a different approach"**: "85% of your students aren't meeting math standards with current methods. ModelIt gives students a reason to USE math - they build computational models of systems they care about. It's not another worksheet; it's applied mathematics."

2. **"Desert as classroom"**: "Your students live surrounded by one of the most remarkable ecosystems in California. ModelIt turns Anza-Borrego from a field trip destination into a data source. Students model desert water cycles, animal populations, plant adaptations, solar energy - all using math."

3. **"Camp Borrego + ModelIt"**: "Your 5th graders already spend 3 days at Camp Borrego learning about the desert. ModelIt can turn that experience into a semester-long project where students build computational models of what they observed. The camp becomes the data collection phase of a scientific investigation."

4. **"Bilingual by design"**: "With 87% Hispanic students, language shouldn't be a barrier to computational thinking. ModelIt is fully bilingual - students can build models in Spanish or English. For your EL students, it's mathematical reasoning without the language bottleneck."

5. **"CTE connection"**: "Your agriculture students can model crop yields and water use. Your renewable energy students can model solar panel efficiency. ModelIt makes CTE more rigorous by adding computational modeling to hands-on skills."

6. **"$30K per student - invest in what works"**: "Your district spends $30K per student - well above the state average. The question isn't whether you can afford ModelIt. It's whether you're investing those dollars in tools that actually move the needle on math proficiency. A $5-10K investment in ModelIt could serve every student in the district."

7. **"We'll make the drive"**: "Most vendors don't serve Borrego Springs because it's remote. We're 90 minutes away in Carlsbad, and we'll come to you. In-person training, classroom visits, ongoing support - we believe rural students deserve the same tools as suburban ones."

## Objection Handling

| # | Objection | Response |
|---|-----------|----------|
| 1 | "We're too small to matter to a technology company." | "You're exactly who we built ModelIt for. NSF funded this to reach underserved students. With 345 students, we can implement district-wide for $5-10K and track real impact. Small districts are where we can make the biggest difference per dollar." |
| 2 | "Our internet/tech infrastructure is limited." | "ModelIt is web-based and designed to work on basic devices. Let us visit and check your setup - we can advise on what you need. Most of our models run on a standard Chromebook." |
| 3 | "We don't have a STEM coordinator or dedicated science leadership." | "That's actually simpler for us. We work directly with you and your science teachers. No committee, no bureaucracy. You decide, your teachers implement, we support." |
| 4 | "Our students struggle with basic math - they're not ready for modeling." | "ModelIt IS how they learn basic math. Students who struggle with abstract math often succeed when they can see the math working in a real system. Building a model of desert rainfall isn't an advanced exercise - it's multiplication, ratios, and graphs in a context that matters." |
| 5 | "We already partner with the Anza-Borrego Foundation for science." | "Perfect - ModelIt amplifies that partnership. Your students collect data and make observations during field trips. ModelIt lets them turn those observations into testable computational models. It's observation plus analysis." |
| 6 | "Budget is tight despite the per-pupil numbers." | "Let me help identify the right funding line. With 87% Hispanic and 57% economically disadvantaged students, your LCFF supplemental and Title I allocations include funds specifically for improving math outcomes. ModelIt fits squarely in that category." |
| 7 | "We've tried tech tools before and they didn't stick." | "What makes ModelIt different is it's not a consumption tool - students BUILD things. And we're close enough to provide ongoing support. Monthly check-ins, classroom visits when needed. We won't just sell and disappear." |

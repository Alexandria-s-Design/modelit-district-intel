## What This Means for ModelIt

### Positioning Strategy

ModelIt should be positioned as the **computational modeling bridge** between students' hands-on desert ecology experiences and NGSS-aligned mathematical reasoning:

1. **Desert ecosystem modeling**: Students experience the desert firsthand through Anza-Borrego programs → ModelIt lets them model those ecosystems computationally (water cycles in arid environments, predator-prey in desert food webs, solar energy systems)
2. **Math intervention through science**: With 15% math proficiency, science-integrated math is critical → ModelIt applies mathematical thinking to real desert systems students already know
3. **Bilingual access**: 87% Hispanic students need Spanish-language access → ModelIt is fully bilingual
4. **CTE connection**: Renewable energy pathway → students can model solar energy systems, agricultural water use, tourism carrying capacity

### How ModelIt Complements Current Tools

| Current Tool/Program | What It Does | What ModelIt Adds |
|---------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| Anza-Borrego Field Trips | Hands-on desert observation | Computational models of observed ecosystems |

| Robotics/Coding | Physical computing & programming | Systems-level modeling (different cognitive skill) |
| CTE Agriculture | Farming/ranching skills | Agricultural systems models (water use, crop yields, sustainability) |
| CTE Renewable Energy | Solar/clean energy | Energy systems models (solar panel efficiency, grid dynamics) |

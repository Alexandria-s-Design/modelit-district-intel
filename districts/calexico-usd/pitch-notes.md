## Key Talking Points

1. **The "Nearly Met" insight** — "At every CAST grade level in Calexico (5, 8, and 11), the largest single group of students scored 'Nearly Met' — 57% to 66%. These students are not failing; they need a different kind of science experience. ModelIt gives them hands-on computational modeling to cross the line."

2. **Bilingual by design** — "ModelIt is the only K-12 science modeling platform with full English/Spanish bilingual support. In a community where 99% of students are Hispanic and many families speak Spanish at home, this isn't a feature — it's a requirement. Your superintendent grew up in this community. He understands why."

3. **NSF-funded credibility** — "We are NSF SBIR Phase II funded — the same federal program that funds serious science research. We've been peer-reviewed, field-tested, and published. We're not just another EdTech startup."

4. **NGSS alignment** — "ModelIt directly addresses Science & Engineering Practice 2 (Developing and Using Models) and Practice 5 (Using Mathematics and Computational Thinking) — the two practices most frequently cited as gaps in CAST performance for districts like Calexico."

5. **Complements, doesn't replace** — "We do NOT replace your existing science curriculum — whatever it is. ModelIt is the hands-on computational layer that goes on top. If you use FOSS, we complement FOSS. If you use Amplify, we complement Amplify. If you haven't yet committed to a science curriculum, we're ready to anchor it."

6. **Greenfield opportunity** — "We see in your board meeting record and district website that Calexico has not yet publicly committed to a specific K-12 science curriculum publisher. That means you can choose tools that truly fit your students — not tools that were designed for a different demographic."

7. **Measure Q STEM buildings = right moment** — "You've invested $47M in new STEM facilities. When those buildings open, teachers will walk into labs that need modern digital tools. We want to be the modeling platform in that environment from day one."

8. **Title IV-A funding pathway** — "ModelIt purchases can be funded through Title IV-A (Student Support and Academic Enrichment grant). Calexico qualifies as a Title I district. This is discretionary federal money that is explicitly allowed for STEM educational technology — no new budget required."

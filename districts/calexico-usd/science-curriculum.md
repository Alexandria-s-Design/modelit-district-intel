**Critical gap**: Despite California's NGSS adoption in 2013, Calexico Unified has not publicly confirmed any branded NGSS-aligned curriculum (STEMscopes, Amplify Science, Discovery, etc.). This suggests either:
- a) Using older, non-NGSS-aligned materials
- b) Using teacher-developed or free resources
- c) A recent curriculum is in place but not publicized

**Supporting evidence for gap**: CAST proficiency rates of 10–14% at all grade levels; domain-level data shows 65–70% of students below standard across all three science domains (Life, Physical, Earth/Space).

## Sales Opportunity Analysis

### Why Calexico USD Needs a Science Tool Now

1. **"Nearly Met" cohort dominates**: 57–66% of students at every tested grade are one tier below proficiency. These students have foundational knowledge but lack the interactive, inquiry-based learning that drives performance to the met/exceeded threshold.

2. **New STEM facilities creating demand**: As STEM buildings complete at CHS in 2025, there is a natural "we need tools for our new labs" opening. The district invested $65.4M in physical infrastructure — digital curriculum is the logical next investment.

3. **No confirmed science curriculum**: Unlike districts that have locked-in multi-year contracts, Calexico appears to have NO dominant science curriculum vendor. This is a greenfield opportunity.

4. **60% English Learner population**: ModelIt's bilingual interface addresses the EL proficiency gap directly — EL science proficiency is likely below even the district's 12% average.

5. **After-school STEM is established**: SPARKs program with 1,000+ students already has STEM enrichment. A science simulation tool fits naturally as an upgrade to existing Lego/chess activities.

6. **Technology-committed leadership**: Superintendent Jimenez explicitly positions technology as central to the district's educational mission. The district built a Technology Committee and integrates tech into LCAP planning.

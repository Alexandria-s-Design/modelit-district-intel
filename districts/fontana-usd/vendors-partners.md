# Fontana Unified School District - Vendors & Partners

**Last Updated**: 2026-02-11
**District Size**: ~33,039 students, 44 schools (K-12)
**Procurement Portal**: OpenGov eProcurement (https://www.fusd.net/departments/business-services-division/purchasing/opengov)

> **Large Urban District Note**: FUSD is a 44-school district serving ~33,000 students in Fontana (Inland Empire). The vendor ecosystem is significantly larger than smaller pipeline districts. The district uses OpenGov for formal procurement and BoardDocs for board-approved contracts.

## Vendor Landscape Analysis

### Where ModelIt Fits

| Position | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Direct competitors present** | None identified -- no computational modeling platform in use |
| **Complementary tools** | Discovery Ed (content) + i-Ready (assessment) + makerspaces (hardware) → ModelIt adds computational modeling layer |
| **Gap ModelIt fills** | The "thinking" between making and testing -- students have tools to BUILD things and tools to TEST knowledge, but nothing to MODEL systems computationally |
| **Price sensitivity** | LOW-MEDIUM -- $722M revenue, $132M Supp/Conc, $408M bond; district has resources |
| **Procurement process** | OpenGov eProcurement for formal bids; Board approval for contracts above threshold |

### Warm Intro Paths

1. **Dr. Alejandro Alvarez** (Rialto USD Superintendent): Spent entire early career at Fontana USD (Tokay teacher through Assoc. Supt. of Business Services). Can make warm introductions to current leadership including business services and instruction.

2. **Dolly Bergen** (STEAM Coordinator): Direct entry to STEM curriculum decisions. Position ModelIt as the computational modeling complement to Discovery Education.

3. **Kim Lawe, Ed.D.** (CTE Director): CTE funding stream (CTEIG, Perkins, SWP) is separate from instructional budget. ModelIt fits Engineering, Biotech, Mechatronics pathways.

4. **Danielle Holley** (Board Member): Former FUSD teacher with MA in Curriculum & Instruction. Fontana Chamber Exec Director. Understands both education and business case.

5. **Discovery Education relationship**: FUSD already buys Discovery Ed. ModelIt can position as the next layer: "You have great content. Now let students BUILD with it."

### Competitive Threats

| Threat | Level | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Status quo (no new tools) | LOW | Math crisis demands action; $408M bond creates urgency |
| Discovery Education expansion | MEDIUM | If Discovery Ed expands into modeling/simulation, overlap could emerge |
| Budget deficit ($14M projected) | MEDIUM | Fiscal pressure from ESSER cliff; but Supp/Conc and bond are separate streams |
| Procurement complexity (large district) | MEDIUM | OpenGov eProcurement, board approval thresholds, RFP processes for large purchases |
| Competing vendor pitches | MEDIUM | Large district attracts many vendors; need to differentiate clearly |
| Teacher adoption resistance | LOW | WRO robotics culture and makerspace culture suggest STEM-positive teachers |

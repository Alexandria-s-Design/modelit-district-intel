## The Hook

> "Nuview Union invested solar energy savings to build a STEM Lab at Mountain Shadows that serves 120 students a day — including modules on sustainable agriculture and oceanography. But 78% of your students still aren't meeting math standards, and your K-5 science curriculum predates NGSS. ModelIt bridges both gaps. Your STEM Lab students can take their hands-on agriculture modules and BUILD computational models of crop yields and water systems. Your elementary students get NGSS-aligned modeling practice — SEP #2, Developing and Using Models — that Harcourt Science doesn't provide. And with 85% Hispanic students and 24% English Learners, ModelIt's fully bilingual interface means every student can build models in English or Spanish."

## Decision Process

In a small K-8 district:

1. **Director of Ed Services evaluates** → Erica Williams reviews ModelIt for curriculum fit and NGSS alignment
2. **Superintendent approves** → John Huber signs off on tool and budget
3. **CBO confirms funding** → Seth Heeren identifies Title I, LCFF Supplemental, or ELOP funds
4. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold
5. **Implementation** → Mountain Shadows STEM Lab (7-8) as starting point, expand to 5-6 at elementary schools

**No formal RFP** expected for a small pilot.

## Vendor Landscape Analysis

### Where ModelIt Fits

| Position | Assessment |
|----------|-----------|
| **Direct competitors present** | None identified — pre-NGSS curriculum with no modeling tools |
| **Complementary tools** | STEM Lab (hands-on), ENGIE solar (energy data), PALI (outdoor ed) |
| **Gap ModelIt fills** | NGSS-aligned computational modeling — currently absent from the district |
| **Price sensitivity** | HIGH — per-pupil spending below state median. Must position within existing funding streams. |

### Warm Intro Paths

1. **ENGIE/OpTerra connection**: The ENGIE partnership showed the district how to redirect energy savings to STEM. ModelIt can position within this "invest savings in STEM" framework.

2. **RCOE (Riverside County Office of Education)**: County office may have STEM coordinators who can introduce ModelIt to multiple Riverside County districts.

3. **PALI Institute**: If PALI provides outdoor science experiences, ModelIt can extend those into computational modeling back in the classroom.

### Competitive Threats

| Threat | Level | Rationale |
|--------|-------|-----------|
| Status quo (pre-NGSS curriculum) | HIGH | Inertia is the biggest threat. District may be comfortable with Harcourt. |
| Budget constraints | HIGH | Below-median per-pupil spending; district is resource-constrained |
| NGSS adoption consuming all science budget | MEDIUM | If/when they adopt new NGSS core curriculum, the supplemental budget may be exhausted |
| Free alternatives (PhET, etc.) | LOW | Free sims don't offer model-building |
| Charter HS drawing attention/resources | LOW | Charter is a separate focus |

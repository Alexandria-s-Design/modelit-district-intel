## The Hook

> "Panama-Buena Vista's junior high students score only 22% in math and 26% in science — but your third graders score 43% in math. That gap isn't a student problem; it's an instruction problem in grades 6–8. ModelIt gives junior high science and math teachers a research-backed, bilingual computational modeling platform that rebuilds systems thinking at exactly the grade band where P-BVUSD students are falling behind."

**Alternate Hook (EL-focused):**
> "With 14.5% English Learners scoring just 7.48% on the CAST science exam, P-BVUSD has the clearest science equity gap in Kern County. ModelIt is the only bilingual (English/Spanish) computational modeling platform built for NGSS — and it's NSF SBIR Phase II funded, peer-reviewed, and ready to pilot at any of your five junior high schools."

## Decision Process

1. **Director of ACT** (Dr. Valerie Park) reviews and recommends curriculum materials
2. **Asst. Supt. Instructional Services** (Trina Lovio) approves with budget context
3. **Superintendent** (Dr. Katie Russell) endorses for board action (if >$25K typically)
4. **Board of Education** approves by consent or action item (2nd Tuesday monthly)
5. **Business Services** (Asst. Supt. Katie Gonzalez) executes contract

> **Shortcut path:** A pilot under $10K may not require board approval — principals can approve at school site level. Target a single JHS (e.g., Warren or Thompson) for a free trial or minimal-cost pilot to build district evidence before district-wide contract proposal.

## 5-Phase Approach

### Phase 1: Open the Door (Weeks 1–2)
- Email Dr. Valerie Park, Jill Bunch, and IS Coordinators simultaneously
- Subject line: "Bilingual science modeling platform — 26% CAST, 22% Grade 8 math in P-BVUSD"
- Attach one-pager and 2-minute demo video link
- Offer a 20-minute Zoom + Q&A — no sales pressure

### Phase 2: First Meeting — Data & Demo (Weeks 3–4)
- 20–30 minute Zoom with Dr. Park (primary) + any IS Coordinators available
- Show: PBVUSD-specific CAST and math data side-by-side with state average
- Demo: Live student modeling session (junior high Earth/life science model)
- Ask: "What does your current science program look like in grades 6–8?"
- Ask: "What would a low-risk pilot look like for your team?"

### Phase 3: School-Site Engagement (Weeks 5–8)
- Request introduction to 1–2 JHS principals (Warren, Thompson, Stonecreek as top picks)
- Coordinate with IS Coordinator for a teacher demo day at target JHS
- Identify 1–2 science teachers willing to pilot 4–6 week unit
- Provide free teacher training session (2 hours, in-person or Zoom)

### Phase 4: Pilot Proposal (Month 3)
- Submit formal pilot proposal to Trina Lovio and Dr. Park
- Frame as: 1 school, 1 semester, 2–3 classes, full PD included
- Proposed cost: $8K–$12K (under single-site approval threshold if applicable)
- Budget pathway: LCFF Supplemental or Title I
- Success metrics: pre/post CAST-aligned assessment, teacher satisfaction, student engagement

### Phase 5: District Expansion (Month 6–12)
- Present pilot results to IS leadership + Asst. Supts.
- Propose district-wide JHS license (all 5 JHS + 2 TK-8 schools)
- Estimated district-wide deal: $50K–$100K depending on scope
- Board approval item: June board meeting for next year implementation

## LCFF Funding Pathway Analysis

### Why This District Gets Significant Supplemental/Concentration Funds
California's LCFF provides **Supplemental** and **Concentration** grant funding for "unduplicated pupils" — English Learners, Foster Youth, and Socioeconomically Disadvantaged (SED) students.

| LCFF Category | Estimated Rate | P-BV Union Relevance |
|---------------|---------------|---------------------|
| Base Grant (all students) | ~$9,000–$10,500/pupil | 19,366 students |
| Supplemental Grant (+20%) | 20% of base for unduplicated % | 62.1% Hispanic + 14.5% EL = high unduplicated count |
| Concentration Grant (+50%) | 50% of base for >55% unduplicated | Likely triggers concentration grant |

> **Insight:** If SED is substantially higher than the 14.3% CDE baseline (likely), combined with 14.5% EL, the district's "unduplicated pupil percentage" likely exceeds 65%, triggering both Supplemental AND Concentration grants. This creates meaningful discretionary LCFF dollars that can fund instructional tools aligned to LCAP goals.

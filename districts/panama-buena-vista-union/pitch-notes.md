## 7 Key Talking Points

1. **The math cliff is real and visible in PBVUSD data.**
   > "Your district shows one of the sharpest math proficiency drops in Kern County — from 43% in Grade 3 to 22% in Grade 8. ModelIt's computational modeling approach rebuilds the systems thinking that collapses between elementary and junior high."

2. **Science scores are below where they could be — without a known cause.**
   > "26% on CAST is low, but there's no clear evidence of an NGSS-aligned science curriculum in place. That's an open opportunity — ModelIt can be the computational backbone that any science program lacks."

3. **Bilingual platform for a bilingual community.**
   > "With 62% Hispanic enrollment and 14.5% English Learners, P-BVUSD is a bilingual community. ModelIt is the only computational science modeling platform built in both English and Spanish. That's not a feature — for students scoring 7% on CAST, it's the difference."

4. **NSF SBIR Phase II — peer reviewed, not a startup gamble.**
   > "ModelIt is NSF SBIR Phase II funded, which means independent scientists have reviewed the academic rigor. This isn't a vendor pitch — it's a research-validated tool you can present to your board with confidence."

5. **Complements, never replaces.**
   > "You've invested in McGraw Hill and HMH across math and ELA. ModelIt doesn't compete — it extends those skills into science investigation. Teachers appreciate that it fits alongside their existing curriculum, not instead of it."

6. **Junior high is the highest-leverage pilot site.**
   > "Five junior high schools, CAST administered at Grade 8, and the biggest proficiency gap in the district. A single-school pilot with two science classes can generate statistically meaningful pre/post data within one semester."

7. **Students love it because it feels like a video game.**
   > "Students also have access to MicroMayhem — a game where students battle real biological threats using the same modeling skills they learn in class. Students love it because it feels like a video game. Teachers love it because every move reinforces real science."

## Objection Handling

| Objection | Response |
|-----------|---------|
| "We already have a science curriculum." | "ModelIt doesn't replace it — it adds the computational modeling layer that NGSS SEP2 and SEP5 require, but that most core science texts don't provide. What does your current program look like for grades 6–8?" |
| "Our budget is tight this year." | "Understood — that's why we've helped districts like yours access LCFF Supplemental and Title I funds specifically for tools that serve high-needs students. With your EL population scoring 7% on CAST, this fits those funding priorities precisely." |
| "We don't have devices for all students." | "You already have ClassLink, Canvas, and Google Workspace deployed district-wide, which means device access is in place. ModelIt runs in any browser — no installation required." |
| "We tried a new science tool and teachers didn't use it." | "That's a common outcome when tools don't fit teacher workflow. ModelIt includes structured PD built into the pilot — your teachers receive in-person or virtual training before any student-facing rollout. Usage rate is our shared success metric." |
| "We need board approval for new vendors." | "We can structure an initial pilot under the site-based approval threshold — typically a single-school trial that lets you generate data before bringing a district-wide proposal to the board. That's a much easier path." |
| "How does this compare to Amplify?" | "Amplify is a full curriculum replacement — a significant lift for teachers and a large budget commitment. ModelIt is an add-on that costs a fraction of a full curriculum adoption, works alongside whatever program you have, and specifically addresses the computational modeling gap that Amplify doesn't fill." |
| "We have other priorities right now." | "I understand — that's exactly why we're proposing a low-lift pilot. One teacher, two classes, one semester. You don't have to reorganize anything. We bring the training, the platform, and the data collection. You get results you can use at your next LCAP review." |
| "We've never heard of ModelIt." | "That's why the NSF SBIR Phase II grant matters — it means the federal government's science research arm has peer-reviewed and funded it. We're a small company with serious academic backing, and we work specifically in districts with the demographic profile of P-BVUSD because that's where the research need is greatest." |

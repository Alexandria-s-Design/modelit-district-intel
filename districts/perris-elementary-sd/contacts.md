## Key Relationships & Strategic Notes

- **Two STEM-branded schools are the key**: Railway (Math/Science/Technology) and Clearwater (STEAM) are purpose-built for ModelIt. Start here.
- **Small district = personal relationships**: 9 schools, ~5,500 students means superintendent, principals, and CBO are all accessible by phone and email.
- **Carol Jimenez dual role**: She is both a board trustee (Clerk) AND district staff (Director, Child Development). This is rare and makes her an exceptionally powerful champion — she understands both the instructional and governance sides.
- **Innovative Horizons Charter autonomy**: Charter schools within a district can often adopt tools independently, bypassing the full district procurement process. This is the fastest pilot path.
- **High ED population = strong funding case**: 83.7% economically disadvantaged = Title I intensive + maximum LCFF Supplemental concentration funding. The budget case is strong.
- **100% minority enrollment**: Bilingual Spanish interface is not a nice-to-have — it's a necessity. Lead with equity angle.
- **Four board seats up in Dec 2026**: Time a pilot success story to give board members a STEM win before re-election.
- **Riverside County context**: District is in RCOE jurisdiction. A successful PESD pilot can be leveraged with neighboring RCOE districts (Perris UHSD, Val Verde USD).

## Key Talking Points

1. **"83.7% economically disadvantaged"**: "Nearly all of your students qualify as economically disadvantaged. Title I funds MUST support math intervention. ModelIt fits perfectly within that allocation."
2. **"Riverside County rural community"**: "Perris Elementary serves rural Perris area. ModelIt lets students build models of systems they observe locally — agriculture, water, weather — making math relevant to their lives."
3. **"100% minority enrollment"**: "Your district serves a diverse student population. ModelIt's bilingual interface ensures equity in access for every student."
4. **"K-6 is ModelIt's sweet spot"**: "Grades 5-6 are where computational modeling fits developmentally. Your district IS grades K-6 — perfect alignment."
5. **"Not just clicking through simulations"**: "Free platforms like PhET let students click through pre-built activities. ModelIt lets students BUILD models — that's the difference between consumers and creators of knowledge."
6. **"School-level principal decision maker"**: "With 8 schools, principals control instructional decisions. Principal Pebly at Perris Elementary influences 25% of district enrollment."
7. **"NGSS computational thinking"**: "California standards emphasize computational thinking. ModelIt develops this practice through model-building — Science and Engineering Practice #2."

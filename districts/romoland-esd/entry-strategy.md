## The Hook

> "With 15.3% math proficiency — 19 points below state average — Romoland's students are facing a math crisis. Your district emphasizes hands-on learning through manipulative mathematics and laboratory-based science. ModelIt adds the missing computational layer. Your students go from concrete manipulatives to building their own computational models — that's the transition from NGSS SEP #3 (Planning and Carrying Out Investigations) to SEP #2 (Developing and Using Models). And with 72% Hispanic students and 30% English Learners, ModelIt's fully bilingual interface means every student can build models in English or Spanish — not just clicking through pre-built simulations, but reasoning mathematically about real systems."

## Decision Process

In a K-8 district:
1. **Director of Ed Services evaluates** → Dr. Echiverri reviews ModelIt for curriculum fit and NGSS alignment
2. **Asst. Superintendent champions** → Dr. Wise champions to Superintendent for budget support
3. **Superintendent approves** → Trevor Painter signs off on tool and budget
4. **CBO confirms funding** → Karen Owen identifies Title I, LCFF Supplemental, or Title III funds
5. **Board approval** → Only if purchase exceeds district threshold
6. **Implementation** → Romoland Middle School (7-8) as starting point, expand to 5-6 at elementary schools

**No formal RFP** expected for a small pilot.

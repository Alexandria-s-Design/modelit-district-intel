## Key Talking Points

1. **"19 points below state average in math"**: "Only 15.3% of Romoland's students are meeting math standards. That's 19 points below California's average. ModelIt makes math meaningful through applied modeling — students use algebra and graphs to build models of real systems they care about."
2. **"Hands-on needs a computational layer"**: "Your district already values hands-on learning with manipulative mathematics and laboratory-based science. ModelIt extends that from concrete to computational. Students go from touching objects to building models — that's NGSS SEP #2."
3. **"72% Hispanic means bilingual isn't optional"**: "With 72.2% Hispanic students and 29.7% English Learners, your district needs instructional tools that work in both languages. ModelIt is fully bilingual — every button, label, and instruction in English and Spanish."
4. **"92.4% economically disadvantaged"**: "Nearly all of your students qualify as economically disadvantaged. Title I funds MUST support math intervention. ModelIt fits perfectly within that allocation."
5. **"Modeling vs. clicking"**: "Free simulations like PhET let students click through pre-built activities. ModelIt lets students BUILD models — that's the mathematical and computational thinking NGSS demands. That's the difference between consumers and creators of knowledge."
6. **"Middle school is the entry point"**: "ModelIt's strongest fit is grades 5-8. Romoland Middle serves grades 7-8 — perfect for a pilot that can then expand to grades 5-6."
7. **"Suburban district growing enrollment"**: "Romoland's enrollment is growing. Your community is investing in education. A pilot that demonstrates results can scale district-wide."
